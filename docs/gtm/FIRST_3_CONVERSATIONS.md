# First 3 Conversations — validate the core assumption before the 15

**Purpose:** before investing in 15 interviews, confirm the core assumption holds at
all with **3 high-quality, learning-only conversations.** This is the cheapest way
to find out we're wrong.

**Hard rules — this is learning, not selling:**
- ❌ No pitch · ❌ No demo · ❌ No product walkthrough · ❌ No pricing.
- ✅ Open questions, then listen. Talk <20% of the time.
- The moment you explain what you're building, you've contaminated the signal.

**Product stays frozen.** No Learning / Prevention / Allocation / AI / Agents /
Prediction. No PR.

---

## Who — 3 angles on the same gap

| Persona | Why them | Their view of the gap |
|---|---|---|
| **1 · Customer Success leader** | owns onboarding/activation | sees accounts stall after signing, chases them manually |
| **1 · CRO / VP Sales** | owns the number | sees deals marked "won" — but does the second invoice land? who owns it after handoff? |
| **1 · CEO / COO** | owns the whole flow | can anyone tell them what's lost between signature and activation, and who owns it? |

Three roles, one question, independent vantage points. If the same pain surfaces
from all three *unprompted*, it's real.

---

## The core question (every conversation)

> **"What happens between a signed contract and the second invoice?"**

Ask it, then stop talking. Let them narrate the whole journey in their own words.

**Per-persona opener — ask THIS, then go quiet. Same gap, three angles:**
- **CS leader:** *"What happens at your company between signing the contract and the
  second invoice?"*
- **CRO / VP Sales:** *"How many customers sign but don't reach value fast enough?"*
- **CEO / COO:** *"Where do you lose revenue after the deal is already closed?"*

That's the whole opener. Do not add to it, do not explain why you're asking. Let them
fill the silence with their own world.

---

## Listen for STORIES, not answers

You are not collecting answers — you are collecting **stories.** The signal is when
they say things, unprompted, like:

- *"we have customers who fall through onboarding"*
- *"it's not always clear who owns it"*
- *"sometimes customers just disappear"*
- *"the renewal doesn't come in on time"*
- *"everyone's sure someone else is handling it"*
- *"we don't have a real number for it"*

The strongest of all is when **they themselves** arrive at:
*"we don't really know"* · *"nobody really holds this"* · *"we can't quantify it."*
That's the moment you've found something.

**Tally these UNPROMPTED mentions** (only if they raised it without you leading):

- [ ] onboarding delays
- [ ] accounts that never activate
- [ ] renewal / second-invoice leakage
- [ ] ownership gaps ("nobody really owns it")
- [ ] broken handoffs (sales → CS → product)
- [ ] nobody owns the metric
- [ ] can't quantify the revenue impact

The last two are the strongest buy signals — *unowned* and *unmeasured* is the gap
the product fills.

---

## Anti-bias discipline (or the test is worthless)

- **Never say "Revenue Recovery."** Don't mention the product, Proof, or the "OS."
  Don't describe the leak — let *them* name it first.
- Don't ask leading yes/no questions ("do you lose revenue in onboarding?"). Ask
  open ones ("what happens to...").
- Count a pain only if it came up **before** you mentioned it.
- A polite "yeah I guess that happens" ≠ pain. Look for emotion, a story, a number.
- Let them describe *their* world. The job is to discover, not to convince.

---

## Capture sheet (one per conversation)

**Write down what they said WORD FOR WORD.** Not your summary, not your
interpretation — their actual sentences. The verbatim quotes are the evidence we
decide on; a paraphrase has already lost the signal.

```
Persona / role / company / date:
Their story, signed -> second invoice (WORD FOR WORD, their sentences):
Strongest verbatim quotes (the exact phrases that made you lean in):
UNPROMPTED pains (check list above):
Who owns the signed->activated->paid gap, per them:
Is it measured today? (Y/N + how):
Could they quantify the $ lost? (Y/N + any number):
Emotion level (shrug / annoyed / this-is-a-real-problem):
Did they ask "can you help with this?" (unsolicited pull):
Verdict: painful+unowned+measurable?  YES / NO
```

## What kills the idea (stop and sharpen the wedge)

If **all three** independently say the gap is already handled — explicitly:

- [ ] there's a KPI for it
- [ ] there's a clear owner
- [ ] there's a dashboard
- [ ] there's a defined process
- [ ] they measure it every week
- [ ] "this isn't a real problem for us"

→ then there is no wedge here. **Stop. Do not run the 15.** Sharpen or pivot the wedge
before investing any further effort. A clean "no" after 3 calls is a win — it saved
the next twelve.

---

## Decision rule (use real evidence, not hope)

After the 3 conversations, score each as **YES** only if it independently revealed a
problem that is **painful AND unowned AND measurable**.

- **≥ 2 of 3 = YES → continue** to the full 15-conversation validation
  ([`DISCOVERY_SCRIPT.md`](DISCOVERY_SCRIPT.md), [`TARGET_ACCOUNTS.md`](TARGET_ACCOUNTS.md)).
- **The problem comes back as already owned, measured, and adequately solved →
  reassess** the wedge before investing further. Do not build; rethink.
- **Mixed / weak signal → run 1–2 more** before deciding; don't force a conclusion
  from a tie.

The point of these three calls is to earn the right to do the next twelve — or to
save the effort. Either outcome is a win, because both are *truth*.
