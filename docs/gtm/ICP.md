# Ideal Customer Profile — Activation Recovery

We are not looking for "a SaaS company." We are looking for a company where money
**provably** leaks between **signature and the second invoice**, where someone
already owns that pain, and where the result can be **measured**.

## The profile (all should be true)

| Dimension | Target | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Model | **B2B SaaS subscription** | recurring invoices = a real money event to anchor proof |
| Size | **$2M–50M ARR** | big enough that the leak is real $, small enough to move fast |
| ACV | **~$10–50k / account** | enough revenue per account that one save is worth the work |
| Onboarding | **Complex (14–90 days)** | complexity = stalls = the leak we recover |
| Customer Success | **CS / onboarding team exists** | the pain already has an owner and a budget line |
| Renewals | **Renewals / second invoices exist** | the cash event that turns recovery into proof |
| Activation | **A definable activation event** | "aha" must be nameable, or we can't detect the stall |

## Champion (who feels the pain first)

**VP Customer Success · Head of Onboarding · RevOps.** They are measured on
activation / time-to-value / net revenue retention and already lose sleep over
signed-but-stalled accounts. **Not the CFO first** — the CFO validates budget after
the owner is sold.

## Qualifying signals (lean in)

- They can name their activation event and roughly what % of signups reach it.
- They have a manual, heroic, unscalable process for chasing stalled accounts.
- Activation/time-to-value is an explicit company metric or OKR.
- Onboarding and billing data live in systems they can already export.

## Disqualifiers (walk away)

- **Self-serve micro-ACV** (<$2k): too little money per account; volume play, not ours.
- **Heavy bespoke enterprise**: onboarding is a 9-month custom project; no cohort.
- **No measurable activation event**: if "activated" is a vibe, we can't detect or prove.
- **Already runs a proven activation-recovery program with attribution**: wrong wedge → pivot.

## Simple fit score (0–7, one point each)

B2B SaaS subscription · $2–50M ARR · $10–50k ACV · complex onboarding · CS exists ·
renewals exist · definable activation event.

- **6–7** → prioritize, request CSV.
- **4–5** → interview, probe the gaps.
- **≤3** → skip.

## Where they cluster (sourcing hints)

Vertical SaaS with implementation/onboarding (fintech ops, healthtech, martech,
devtools with setup, B2B marketplaces), Series A–C, companies hiring "Onboarding
Manager / Implementation / Activation" roles, and those publicly talking about
time-to-value or NRR.
