## Pre-research position (bias firewall — §9 Phase 3, recorded BEFORE analysing the reports)
- **Initial hypothesis:** NH-ROP will materially improve precision/traceability/uncertainty-preservation, and will *pass* validation for L1–L3, with genuine friction (overhead) on low-materiality questions and at least one or two real defects surfaced (ambiguities, missing rules).
- **Initial assumptions:** the six chosen reports are accessible and contain enough material claims; disconfirming evidence is findable via public search.
- **Existing beliefs:** I designed NH-ROP (Mission #002) → **conflict of interest**: I am predisposed to conclude it works. Mitigation: I will actively hunt for False Negatives and Overhead Failures, record every friction event, and treat "no defects found" as a red flag, not a success.
- **Expected answer:** "Validate for L1–L3, with conditions; not yet L4; N improvement recommendations."
- **Potential conflict of interest:** author validating own protocol (the exact Trust-Invariant risk NH-ROP itself warns about). Logged as `M003-A0`.
- **What evidence would change the expected answer:** the protocol proving unusable on a real report (can't decompose, forces invented rules), or producing a verdict *stronger* than evidence that a narrative read would have caught → would move toward "Do not validate / validate L1 only."
