## Pre-research position (bias firewall)
- **Anchor hypothesis (to be tested):** spacing beats massing for long-term retention.
- **Not assumed true:** the anchor was tested against the evidence; verdicts could support, partially
  support, contradict, or reject it. Outcome: mostly **Supported** for the core recall claim, but
  **Partially Supported** for universality and **Inconclusive** for transfer — uncertainty preserved.

## Findings (plain language — pilot record, not an NH position)
The spacing effect (spaced > massed for long-term retention) is **Supported** by a large meta-analysis and an
independent high-utility review (C1/C2); the optimal gap grows with the retention interval (C3). But
**universal** generalization is only **Partially Supported** (C4 — most evidence is verbal/lab); authentic-
setting evidence is comparatively **limited** (C6); transfer/understanding (vs recall) is **Inconclusive**
(C7). Spaced repetition is widely **implemented** in software (C5). See `RESEARCH_RECORD.md`.
